🎯
PLANNER
Parses the query. Detects all intents. Extracts tickers. Builds the data checklist.
▼
  • Calls LLM once to extract TICKERS, INTENTS, TIMEFRAME
  • Supports multiple simultaneous intents: outlook, comparison, catalyst, historical, recommendation
  • Builds checklist = union of all data types required across every detected intent
  • Example: "Compare TSLA vs RIVN on valuation and news" → intents: [comparison, catalyst] → checklist of 8 items
  • Initialises all state fields so every agent starts clean
⟳ Research loop max 3 passes
🔍
RESEARCHER
Works through the checklist. Loops back if items are still missing. Returns for targeted fetches when Moderator requests specific data.
▼
  • Checklist mode (initial): fetches each uncollected item in order
  • Targeted mode (Moderator sent it back): fetches only what was specifically requested
  • stock_info → price, P/E, margins, market cap, analyst mean target
  • earnings → quarterly history, income statement
  • price_history → 1y or 2y depending on intent
  • price_history_5y → longer lookback for historical intent
  • analyst_recommendations → recent upgrades/downgrades
  • news_general → recent headlines (30 days)
  • news_catalyst → "why is X moving" search (14 days)
  • news_outlook → forward estimates, analyst coverage
  • All Tavily results preserve URL + title for final citations
📊
QUANT ANALYST
Analyzes hard numbers independently. No awareness of what Qual will say.
▼
  • Works from research data only — never sees Qual's output
  • P/E, PEG, P/B, margins, ROE, D/E with exact figures
  • Price change % across specific timeframes
  • Earnings acceleration or deceleration trends
  • Valuation vs historical averages and sector
  • If Moderator sent a specific question → addresses it directly
📰
QUAL ANALYST
Analyzes narrative and context independently. No awareness of what Quant said.
▼
  • Works from news and research data — never sees Quant's output
  • Recent news with inline citations [Source: Title](URL)
  • Industry tailwinds and headwinds
  • Management signals: guidance, exec moves, strategy shifts
  • Market sentiment and macro environment
  • If Moderator sent a specific question → addresses it directly
⚖️
MODERATOR — quality gate
Reads both analysts. Finds agreements, conflicts, and gaps. Decides what happens next. Owns the loop — not a round counter.
▼
  • Identifies what both analysts agree on (high-confidence conclusions)
  • Identifies conflicts and makes a call on who is right and why
  • Identifies gaps: important questions neither analyst answered
  • Then decides one of three things (see decision table below)
  • Safety cap: if cycle_count ≥ 5, forces VERDICT regardless
  • In practice should converge in 1–2 cycles as gaps are filled
Moderator Decision — one of three routes
NEED_DATA
A specific data point is missing that would resolve a key disagreement or gap
→ Researcher ↺
NEED_ANALYSIS
Data exists but analysts didn't address a specific important question
→ Quant + Qual ↺
VERDICT
Both analysts have covered all key aspects. Confident enough to write report.
→ Writer ✓
↺ NEED_DATA loops to Researcher ↺ NEED_ANALYSIS loops to Quant+Qual
📄
WRITER
Produces the final report. Sections adapt to detected intents. Cites sources inline. Self-evaluates — routes back to Moderator if weak.
▼
  • Executive Summary → direct answer upfront, no burying the lede
  • comparison intent → Head-to-Head Metrics table
  • catalyst intent → "What's Driving Price Action" section, all cited
  • outlook intent → Forward Outlook with EPS estimates and analyst targets
  • recommendation intent → Bull case vs Bear case
  • Always included: Quant vs Qual key agreements + conflicts
  • Always included: Conclusion with confidence level
  • Always included: numbered Sources section with full URLs
  • Self-evaluation: if WEAK → routes back to Moderator for another cycle
COMPLETE
END
